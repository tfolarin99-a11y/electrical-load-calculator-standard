🔄 Recommended Tab Workflow
  • 📋
    Tab 1 — ProjectAlways start here. System voltage and phase setting affects every other module's formulas.
  • 📝
    Tab 2 — LoadsThe foundation of all calculations. Add every load circuit — use presets as a starting point to save time.
  • 📊
    Tab 3 — DemandApply your demand factor. For offices use 0.75; residential 0.70; hospitals 0.80. Add a growth margin (default 20%).
  • ⚡
    Tab 4 — CurrentClick "Import from Demand" — this auto-fills the kW and PF so you don't have to retype values.
  • 〰
    Tab 5 — CablesEnter the design current (use Tab 4's "Design ×1.25" value), select material, installation method and run length.
  • 🔌
    Tab 6 — BreakersEnter full load current. The 125% rule is applied automatically for motor and continuous loads.
  • 📉
    Tab 7 — Voltage DropUse the cable size from Tab 5. A PASS/FAIL status shows against the IEC 3% or 5% limit.
  • 🔋
    Tab 8 — GeneratorClick "Import from Demand". Adjust motor start and spare margins. A standard genset kVA is recommended.
  • 🔆
    Tab 9 — InverterFor backup power systems. Enter the loads to back up, backup hours, battery voltage and efficiency.
  • 🔄
    Tab 10 — TransformerClick "Import from Demand". Select primary voltage (e.g. 11kV) and secondary (e.g. 415V).
  • 📦
    Tab 11 — BOMAuto-generated bill of materials. Click Export CSV to download a spreadsheet for quotation use.
  • ⚖
    Tab 12 — Phase BalanceClick Analyse to detect phase imbalance. Apply suggested reassignments with one click to improve balance.
  • 🌍
    Tab 13 — Energy / CO₂Set operating hours and select your country's grid emission factor to get annual energy and carbon figures.
  • 💰
    Tab 14 — Utility CostSelect your country tariff preset or enter a custom rate to estimate the monthly electricity bill.
  • 📄
    Tab 15 — ReportClick Generate then Print / PDF. The report includes all summary tables, equipment recommendations, and carbon data.
📘 Module Details
📋
Tab 1 — Project InformationSystem configuration and project metadata
Setup ›

Fill in the project name, client name, location and engineer's name. These appear on the printed report. Most importantly, set your system type (single or three phase) and supply voltage here — this affects every formula across all modules.

Common Voltages
230V (1Φ) · 415V (3Φ) · 400V (3Φ) · 240V (1Φ)
Power Factor Default
0.8 — edit this for your installation type
💡 Tip: Nigeria and most of Africa use 230V single phase / 415V three phase at 50Hz. North America uses 120V / 208V at 60Hz.
📝
Tab 2 — Load EntryBuild your full load schedule
Core ›

This is the most important tab — all other calculations flow from the loads you enter here. Each load row has: Description, Category, Quantity, Unit Watts, Power Factor, Demand Factor and Phase Assignment.

Quick Presets
2-Bed House · Office Floor · School · Hotel · Warehouse
Phase Options
R · Y · B · R+Y+B (balanced 3-phase loads)
Demand Factor
Fraction of time a load is actually running (0.1–1.0)
Total Auto-Sum
Connected load total updates live at the bottom
💡 Tip: Use a preset first to populate sample data, then modify quantities and wattages to match your actual project. This is much faster than starting from scratch.
📊
Tab 3 — Demand CalculationApply demand and diversity factors
Auto ›

Formula: Demand Load = Connected Load × Demand Factor ÷ Diversity Factor. Demand factor accounts for the fact that not all loads run simultaneously at full capacity. The growth margin adds headroom for future expansion.

Office Preset
0.75 demand factor
Hospital Preset
0.80 — higher because critical loads run continuously
Warehouse Preset
0.50 — intermittent industrial loads
Growth Margin
Default 20% — adds to Design kVA for sizing
💡 Tip: The "Design +Margin" kVA figure is what you use for generator and transformer sizing — it already includes your growth headroom.
⚡
Tab 4 — Current CalculationLine and phase current with 125% rule
Formula ›

Automatically switches formula based on your system type: 3Φ: I = P ÷ (√3 × V × PF) or 1Φ: I = P ÷ (V × PF). The "Design ×1.25" value adds the NEC/IEC 125% continuous-load safety factor — this is the value to use for cable and breaker sizing.

💡 Tip: Click the yellow "⬇ Import from Demand" button to auto-fill the kW and PF from Tab 3. Never manually retype these values — it avoids transcription errors.
〰
Tab 5 — Cable SizingIEC cable selection with derating factors
IEC ›

Selects the minimum cable size from IEC reference tables based on your corrected current (after derating for temperature and grouping). Also performs a voltage drop check against your chosen limit.

Installation Method A1
Conduit inside wall — most restrictive (lowest ratings)
Installation Method C
Clipped direct — good airflow, higher ratings
Method E / F
Cable tray / free air — highest current ratings
Ambient Temp
Higher temps derate cable capacity — adjust for site conditions
💡 Tip: In Nigeria and hot climates, set ambient temperature to 40–45°C rather than the default 30°C. This will increase the recommended cable size to compensate for heat derating.
🔌
Tab 6 — Breaker SelectionMCB / MCCB / ACB recommendation
Auto ›

Selects the next standard breaker rating above your required current. For motor and continuous loads, the 125% multiplier is applied before selecting the rating.

MCB Range
6–100A — for lighting and small circuit protection
MCCB Range
100–630A — main distribution boards
ACB Range
630A+ — incoming main switchgear
Motor Load
Select "Motor Load" type for proper 125% sizing
💡 Tip: Always match breaking capacity (Icu) to the available fault level at the installation point. Use 10kA for most distribution boards; 25kA+ near transformers.
📉
Tab 7 — Voltage DropPASS/FAIL check against IEC limits
Check ›

Calculates voltage drop using cable resistance from IEC tables. Shows result in Volts and percentage, with PASS/FAIL against your chosen standard. If the cable from Tab 5 fails, increase the cable size here until it passes.

IEC Preferred Limit
3% for final circuits from distribution board
Acceptable Limit
5% for long sub-mains in large installations
💡 Tip: If you get a FAIL result, go back to Tab 5 and increase the cable size by one or two steps. Long cable runs — common in warehouses and campuses — often require upsizing beyond the current rating alone.
🔋
Tab 8 — Generator SizingGenset kVA recommendation with margins
Sizing ›

Adds a motor start margin (default 25%) for motor inrush current, plus a spare capacity margin (default 20%), then selects the nearest standard genset size. Supports diesel, gas and dual fuel options.

💡 Tip: For sites with many motors (pumps, compressors, lifts), increase the motor start margin to 35–50%. Motor starting current can be 6–8× running current and will trip an undersized generator.
🔆
Tab 9 — Inverter & BatteryBackup power sizing
Backup ›

Calculates inverter kVA size and the total battery bank in Ah. Accounts for inverter efficiency, battery efficiency, and depth of discharge to give real-world usable capacity.

Depth of Discharge
Lead Acid: 50% max · LiFePO4: 80–90% safe DoD
Battery Voltage
48V is most efficient for larger systems (lower current)
💡 Tip: For critical loads only (not whole building), enter just the load you need to keep running during outages. This will size a much more cost-effective battery bank.
⚖
Tab 12 — Phase BalancerDetect and fix phase imbalance
Premium ›

Analyses your load schedule, calculates the percentage imbalance between R, Y and B phases, and suggests specific load reassignments to improve balance. Shows a before/after comparison and neutral current estimate.

Imbalance < 5%
Excellent — no action needed
Imbalance 5–15%
Acceptable — minor reassignment recommended
Imbalance > 25%
Critical — high neutral current, risk of overheating
Apply All Button
Applies all suggested reassignments in one click
💡 Tip: High phase imbalance causes excessive neutral current, overheating of neutral conductors, and voltage imbalance which damages motors. Always aim for under 5% on balanced distribution systems.
🌍
Tab 13 — Energy & CO₂Annual consumption and carbon footprint
Premium ›

Calculates daily, monthly and annual energy consumption in kWh, then converts to tonnes of CO₂ using your country's grid emission factor. Shows how many trees would be needed annually to offset the carbon footprint.

💡 Tip: This module is very useful for green building certifications and sustainability reports. Select your country from the emission factor cards for the most accurate figure.
💰
Tab 14 — Utility CostMonthly electricity bill estimate
Premium ›

Estimates the monthly electricity bill by applying energy tariff, demand charges, fixed charges and taxes. Preset tariffs are available for Nigeria, UK, USA, Ghana, South Africa and UAE. Includes energy-saving recommendations based on your load profile.

💡 Tip: The cost breakdown by category identifies your biggest cost drivers. HVAC and motors are typically 60–70% of industrial bills — use this data to justify efficiency upgrades to your client.
💡 Pro Tips
🔗
Always Use Import Buttons

Every sizing module has an "⬇ Import from Demand" button. Use it — it flows calculated values automatically and prevents manual entry errors.

🏗️
Start with a Preset

Load presets (Office Floor, 2-Bed House, etc.) instantly populate a realistic load schedule. Modify quantities and wattages rather than building from scratch.

🌡️
Adjust for Hot Climates

In Nigeria, UAE and tropical climates, set ambient temperature to 40–45°C in the Cable Sizing tab. IEC default of 30°C will undersize your cables.

📄
Report for Client Proposals

Complete all tabs then generate the report. Use Print → Save as PDF to create a professional deliverable showing load summary, equipment recommendations, and energy figures.

📦
Export BOM for Quoting

The Bill of Materials tab auto-generates a components list. Click Export CSV to open in Excel for pricing and supplier quotation preparation.

⚖️
Balance Phases on Every Project

Run the Phase Balancer on every 3-phase project before finalising. High imbalance causes neutral overheating and motor damage — it is a common site problem that is easy to prevent at design stage.
